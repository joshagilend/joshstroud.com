Email

Email is the way America works. With over 2B email accounts in the wild across Gmail, Microsoft Outlook, and other providers, there are more email accounts than Instagram users, YouTube accounts, and phone numbers. Gmail is pervasive as the #1 way to receive consumer email for free with a simple UX and a useful "job to be done" of sending and receiving email. Email is a web standard accepted by every email provider, every SMTP email server, and every email software vendor. Everyone loves email and getting a good "electronic letter" :) Email makes the world go round :)

Authentication With Email

Tech companies, software providers, and websites of all shapes and sizes use email as the primary and often only way to log in, authenticate users, and send communications and notifications to users of their site. Email evolved over 30 years as a login method without a clear statement of the user authentication problem, and there are now many competing alternatives which have failed to be fully adopted by the market: passkeys, magic links, multifactor SMS, and hardware multifactor authentication. What happens if your email is hacked? Users lose access to accounts like banks, social media, and, well, email, with a simple "Forgot Password" link :) Have we mentioned that consumer services have terrible customer support when you lose access to your email? Never again :)

Email Isn't Free

Email is expensive and commercial - Users have to pay for features like extra storage, additional features, and hosting - Email is not free, and users often lose access to their email or key features of their email when they don't pay, they forget to pay, or they can't pay :) Web sites are free because commercial web site creators want the traffic - Why can't email be free? Email is run by tech giants and controlled by commercial and business interests - The internet and the web might run on email, but email is not free - Email is commercial.

Email Is International

Billions of international email users exclusively rely on commercial personal Gmail accounts to do business, communicate, and connect across a wide variety of languages. Gmail and Wordpress together form the backbone of international marketing and promotion to American consumers and businesses, with users often paying less than $10 a month for $100,000+ of business value for Chinese factories, Indian and Ukranian dev shops, and African scammers. Gmail is considered safer, more reliable, and more useful than paid email accounts, and is shared across sales, marketing, and leadership in an international business. Texting

Texting Landscape Fragmentation

In texting today, there are four competing technologies that make up the modern texting landscape: SMS, iMessage, Android RCS, and Chinese Phone RCS. SMS is dominant as the web standard that every phone uses with messaging, image sharing, and audio sharing features. iMessage is the market leader in the United States for rich messaging including texting, photo and video sharing, audio messaging, iOS native features, and tapbacks. Android RCS is the Android competitor to iMessage used by all Android phones with similar features and a UX marred by major bugs and problems. Chinese phones use a Chinese RCS implementation which is different from Android RCS, and often features Chinese surveillance and tracking unique to China. Together, these four web standards make up the modern texting landscape and are used by 97% of mobile phones in the wild.

Messaging Apps: WhatsApp, Telegram, and "All The Rest" :)

WhatsApp is dominant as the #1 international texting solution worldwide. Traveling to any country internationally means switching to WhatsApp, and sending texts and photos home for free. WhatsApp has a great user experience, and features group messaging, voice messages, and image and video sharing features which make WhatsApp delightful and fun. Internationally, Telegram is also dominant as the "secure" mobile messaging solution to hide from surveillance, government spying and tracking, and ISP snooping. There are over 30 other messaging apps across countries, around the world, which all have 100M+ users featuring custom features, languages, and unique networks of people that use their messaging app every day. Who can forget the zany Viber marketing in 2025? #SupaFund :)

Texting Scams, Spam, And Ham-Handed Cybersecurity Takeovers

Texting and SMS are the single most used cybersecurity attack channel in the world. Everyone has received a scammy text from an international number with a poorly spelled domain linking your phone to a fake PayPal where you can "enter your password and win!" :) Texting scams and spam are the #1 way that consumer user accounts are taken over, and approximately 5% of all consumers in the world have experienced at least 1 essential account takeover in their lifetime. Over $50B is lost from the global consumer and business economy every year by texting scams, spam, and cybersecurity takeovers. #SecureYourPhone :)
